Mauritius

Where nature, culture and luxury meet

← All Destinations

Mauritius sits in the Indian Ocean roughly 2,000 kilometres off the southeast coast of Africa, and it's one of the region's most complete destinations. A former British and French colony, the island blends African, European, Indian and Chinese influences into a unique and welcoming culture. Beyond the famous lagoons and luxury resorts, Mauritius offers dramatic volcanic landscapes, sugar cane plantations and waterfalls.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 10–14 nights

Key Highlights

🐟

Blue Bay Marine Park

One of Africa's finest marine parks, home to spectacular coral gardens and a dazzling variety of reef fish.

🌋

Le Morne Brabant

This UNESCO World Heritage basalt monolith rises dramatically from the lagoon — a cultural symbol and hiking destination.

🎨

Seven Coloured Earths

A geological curiosity near Chamarel — volcanic sand dunes in seven distinct colours that never fully mix.

🍹

Rum Heritage

Mauritius has a rich rum-making tradition. Tour the Chamarel distillery and sample award-winning expressions.

Best Time to Visit

May to December is the cooler, drier season and widely considered the best time to visit. January to April can bring cyclones and heavy rainfall, particularly in February and March.

Example Experiences

  • Snorkel or dive in the crystal-clear waters of Blue Bay Marine Park
  • Hike to the summit of Le Morne Brabant for panoramic lagoon views
  • Tour the Chamarel rum distillery and Seven Coloured Earths on a single day trip
  • Take a catamaran cruise around the coast with stops for swimming and snorkelling
