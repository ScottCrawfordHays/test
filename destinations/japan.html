Japan

Ancient tradition meets modern wonder

← All Destinations

Japan is one of the world's most captivating and unique travel destinations. Ancient temple complexes sit alongside futuristic skylines, while centuries-old tea ceremony traditions coexist with cutting-edge technology. The Japanese approach to hospitality — known as omotenashi — ensures that visitors feel genuinely welcomed at every turn. Whether you're drawn by the neon lights of Tokyo, the serene temples of Kyoto, or the iconic silhouette of Mount Fuji, Japan rewards every kind of traveller.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 12–16 nights

Key Highlights

🗼

Tokyo

The world's most vibrant city — explore neighbourhoods from the iconic Shibuya crossing to the tranquil Yanaka district.

⛩️

Kyoto's Temples

More than 1,600 Buddhist temples and 400 Shinto shrines make Kyoto one of Asia's greatest cultural cities.

🏔️

Mount Fuji

Japan's most iconic landmark — whether climbing it or viewing it from the shores of Lake Kawaguchi, it never disappoints.

🌸

Cherry Blossom Season

Hanami — the art of cherry blossom viewing — transforms Japan into a sea of pink and white every spring.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (late March to early May) brings cherry blossoms and mild temperatures — Japan's most celebrated season. Autumn (September to November) offers spectacular foliage. Summer is hot and humid, while winter is ideal for skiing in Hokkaido and Nagano.

Example Experiences

  • Ride the iconic Shinkansen bullet train between Tokyo and Kyoto
  • Stay a night in a traditional ryokan inn with tatami floors and a kaiseki dinner
  • Explore Kyoto's Arashiyama bamboo grove at dawn before the crowds arrive
  • Participate in a traditional tea ceremony with a certified local tea master
🗓️
