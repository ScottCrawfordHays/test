Vietnam

A country of breathtaking contrasts

← All Destinations

Vietnam stretches over 1,600 kilometres from north to south, and that diversity is one of its greatest assets. Ancient trading towns, dramatic limestone landscapes, bustling modern cities and pristine coastlines all await. Vietnamese culture is rich, welcoming and deeply tied to its landscape — from the rice terraces of Sapa to the lantern-lit streets of Hoi An. Add some of Asia's most exciting cuisine and remarkable value, and it's clear why Vietnam has become one of the most rewarding places to travel.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 12–15 nights

Key Highlights

🚢

Ha Long Bay

Cruise through 1,600 limestone islands rising from emerald waters — one of the world's truly iconic natural landscapes.

🏮

Hoi An Ancient Town

This UNESCO World Heritage city glows with lanterns at night, with tailors, galleries and restaurants at every turn.

🍲

Vietnamese Cuisine

From pho and banh mi to fresh spring rolls, Vietnamese food is celebrated worldwide — and it tastes even better here.

🌾

Sapa Rice Terraces

Trek through spectacular terraced hillsides and stay with local Hmong families for an authentic northern Vietnam experience.

Best Time to Visit

Vietnam's climate varies significantly by region. Central Vietnam (Hoi An, Hue) is best from February to August. The north (Hanoi, Ha Long Bay) is most pleasant from September to November. The south is driest from December to April.

Example Experiences

  • Take a two-night junk cruise through Ha Long Bay's limestone karsts
  • Hire a bicycle and explore Hoi An's ancient quarter at your own pace
  • Join a street food walking tour through Hanoi's Old Quarter at dusk
  • Travel the length of the country on the legendary Reunification Express train
