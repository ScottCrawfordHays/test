Mexico

Ancient civilisations and Caribbean coastlines

← All Destinations

Mexico is one of the world's most rewarding holiday destinations — a country of extraordinary depth, colour and variety. The ancient Maya civilisation left behind some of the world's most spectacular archaeological sites, while Mexico City offers one of the greatest concentrations of museums, markets and street food anywhere on earth. The Caribbean coastline of the Yucatán Peninsula, with its turquoise waters and cenote swimming holes, provides world-class beach time.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 10–14 nights

Key Highlights

🏛️

Ancient Maya Ruins

Chichén Itzá, Tulum and Palenque are among the world's great archaeological sites — the Maya civilisation at its most spectacular.

🏖️

Caribbean Coastline

Playa del Carmen, Tulum and the Riviera Maya offer some of the Americas' most beautiful turquoise waters.

🏊

Cenote Swimming

The Yucatán Peninsula is dotted with thousands of freshwater sinkholes — swimming in them is a uniquely otherworldly experience.

🌮

Food and Culture

Mexican cuisine is a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage — from street tacos to fine dining, food is central to Mexican identity.

Best Time to Visit

November to April is the dry season and the best time for both beach and cultural travel. The rainy season (May to October) brings afternoon showers but also fewer crowds. Hurricane season peaks in September and October.

Example Experiences

  • Watch sunrise over the Pyramid of Kukulcán at Chichén Itzá before the crowds arrive
  • Swim in a cenote in the jungle near Valladolid — a uniquely Mexican experience
  • Explore the street food scene in Mexico City's vibrant Roma and Condesa neighbourhoods
  • Snorkel the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef off the coast of Cozumel
🗓️
