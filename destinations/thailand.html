Thailand

Temples, islands and unforgettable flavours

← All Destinations

Thailand has long been one of the most popular holiday destinations for UK travellers, and it's easy to see why. From the golden spires of Bangkok's temples to the turquoise waters of the southern islands, Thailand offers an extraordinary blend of culture, cuisine and natural beauty. Whether you're drawn to vibrant street markets, peaceful Buddhist monasteries or some of the world's most beautiful beaches, Thailand delivers it all at remarkable value.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 10–14 nights

Key Highlights

🛕

Bangkok's Temples

Explore Wat Pho, Wat Arun and the Grand Palace — a dazzling introduction to Thai culture and history.

🏝️

The Southern Islands

From Koh Samui to Phuket and Koh Lanta, Thailand's islands offer everything from lively beach bars to secluded retreats.

🍜

World-Class Street Food

Thai cuisine is one of the world's great food cultures — from pad thai to papaya salad, eating here is an adventure.

🐘

Ethical Wildlife Encounters

Visit a responsible elephant sanctuary in Chiang Mai for an unforgettable and genuinely ethical wildlife experience.

Best Time to Visit

November to April offers the ideal combination of dry weather and warm temperatures. The southwest monsoon runs from May to October — still warm, but expect afternoon showers. High season (December to February) is busiest and most expensive, particularly on the islands.

Example Experiences

  • Explore the temples and markets of Bangkok over two or three nights
  • Take a longtail boat through the limestone karsts of Phang Nga Bay
  • Spend a morning volunteering at an ethical elephant sanctuary near Chiang Mai
  • Watch the sunset over Koh Samui's famous Fisherman's Village at Na Thon
