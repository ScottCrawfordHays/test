Barbados

Caribbean soul, warmth and character

← All Destinations

Barbados is arguably the most refined of all Caribbean islands — a place where British colonial heritage, vibrant rum culture and spectacular natural beauty combine to create one of the world's most welcoming holiday destinations. The island's west coast offers sheltered, calm turquoise waters ideal for swimming and watersports, while the rugged east coast attracts surfers from around the world.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 10–14 nights

Key Highlights

🌊

West Coast Beaches

The famous Platinum Coast offers clear, calm Caribbean waters with powdery white sand and vibrant beach bars.

🐢

Sea Turtle Encounters

Barbados is one of the Caribbean's great sea turtle destinations — snorkel alongside hawksbill turtles in their natural habitat.

🥃

Rum Heritage

Barbados claims to be the birthplace of rum — tour the historic Mount Gay distillery and sample the island's finest.

🏏

Cricket Culture

Cricket is Barbados's national passion — catching a match at Kensington Oval is a genuinely unmissable cultural experience.

Best Time to Visit

December to June is the dry season and peak travel period, offering the most reliable sunshine. The hurricane season runs from June to November, though Barbados is less exposed than many Caribbean islands. Year-round temperatures average around 29°C.

Example Experiences

  • Swim alongside hawksbill sea turtles off the west coast near Paynes Bay
  • Take a guided tour of historic Bridgetown — a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Watch the sun set with a rum punch in hand at Harbour Lights beach bar
  • Explore the dramatic east coast along Bathsheba and the famous Soup Bowl surf break
