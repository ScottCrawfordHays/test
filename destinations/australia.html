Australia

A continent of extraordinary diversity

← All Destinations

Australia is simply vast — a continent of extraordinary diversity that takes most travellers multiple visits to begin to understand. From the iconic Sydney Opera House to the ancient red landscapes of the outback and the spectacular colours of the Great Barrier Reef, Australia rewards curiosity at every turn. Add world-class cities, unique wildlife found nowhere else on earth, and a culture of outdoor living that's genuinely infectious.

✈️Holiday Destination
🗓️Suggested: 17–21 nights

Key Highlights

🐠

Great Barrier Reef

The world's largest coral reef system stretches more than 2,300 kilometres — a snorkelling and diving paradise.

🦘

Unique Wildlife

Kangaroos, koalas, wombats and platypuses are just a few of the remarkable animals found only in Australia.

🌆

Sydney

The Opera House, Harbour Bridge, Bondi Beach and a vibrant dining scene make Sydney one of the world's great cities.

🏜️

Uluru

Australia's most iconic landmark — a sacred red sandstone monolith rising from the desert floor at the heart of the continent.

Best Time to Visit

Southern cities (Sydney, Melbourne) are best from September to November and March to May. The north and Great Barrier Reef are most accessible from April to October. The outback is most pleasant from April to September.

Example Experiences

  • Snorkel the outer Great Barrier Reef from Cairns on a full-day liveaboard trip
  • Watch the sunrise turn Uluru shades of deep red from a champagne breakfast viewpoint
  • Walk across the iconic Sydney Harbour Bridge with a BridgeClimb guide
  • Road trip the Great Ocean Road from Melbourne, stopping at the Twelve Apostles at sunset
