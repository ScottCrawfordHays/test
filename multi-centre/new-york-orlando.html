Culture, excitement and year-round sunshine

Destination One

New York

The city that never stops surprising you

Destination Two

Orlando, Florida

The theme park capital of the world

The United States has no shortage of iconic travel experiences, and the combination of New York City and Orlando represents two very different sides of American life at their most spectacular. New York is culture, energy and endless possibility — world-class museums, Broadway shows, neighbourhood food scenes and a skyline that never loses its power to impress. Orlando, by contrast, is pure entertainment on a magnificent scale: Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld and year-round Florida sunshine. Together they create a trip that works beautifully for couples, families and first-time USA travellers alike.

✈️Two-Centre Holiday
🗓️Suggested: 12–15 nights

Why These Destinations Work Together

New York and Orlando are connected by regular, affordable domestic flights — typically just two and a half hours apart. Starting in New York is the natural choice: the city provides intellectual and cultural stimulation before you head south to Florida for a different kind of excitement altogether. The contrast between the sophistication of Manhattan and the spectacle of the theme parks is part of what makes this combination so satisfying. Both cities also have excellent direct connections from UK airports.

Example Itinerary

1

New York — 5–7 nights

Begin in Manhattan, where the pace and energy immediately get under your skin. New York rewards walkers — allow time to get genuinely lost in it.

  • Walk the Brooklyn Bridge at golden hour and explore the DUMBO neighbourhood below
  • Visit Central Park in the morning, then the Metropolitan Museum of Art in the afternoon
  • Book a Broadway show — from classic musicals to exciting new productions
  • Walk the High Line from Chelsea to the Meatpacking District
  • Take the free Staten Island Ferry for close-up views of the Statue of Liberty
  • Explore the food scene in Greenwich Village, the Lower East Side or Williamsburg
2

Orlando, Florida — 5–7 nights

Fly south from JFK to Orlando in around two and a half hours. A city built almost entirely around extraordinary entertainment.

  • Spend two days at Walt Disney World — Magic Kingdom, EPCOT and Animal Kingdom
  • Visit Universal Studios and Islands of Adventure — including the Wizarding World of Harry Potter
  • Try the rides and shows at SeaWorld Orlando
  • Visit Kennedy Space Center for a genuinely moving and fascinating day trip
  • Explore Disney Springs or International Drive for evening dining and entertainment
  • End with a day at a local nature reserve or airboat tour through the Florida wetlands

Best Time to Travel

April to June and September to November are the ideal windows for New York, with comfortable temperatures and lower hotel prices than peak summer. Orlando's theme parks are busy year-round, but the best conditions are from October to May — avoiding the intense summer humidity and hurricane season.
