Japan + Vietnam

Asia's greatest contrasts in one journey

← Multi-Centre Holidays
Destination One

Japan

Ancient tradition meets modern wonder

Destination Two

Vietnam

A country of breathtaking contrasts

Japan and Vietnam are two of Asia's most distinctive and rewarding travel experiences, and combining them creates a journey of genuine depth and variety. Japan is precise, polished and endlessly surprising — a country where bullet trains run to the second, where ramen shops operate with quiet obsession, and where a visit to a centuries-old Kyoto temple can feel like stepping outside time entirely. Vietnam, by contrast, is raw, warm and full of colour: Ha Long Bay's other-worldly limestone karsts, the lantern-draped streets of Hoi An, and a street food culture that is genuinely world class. Together they capture Asia at its most compelling.

✈️Two-Centre Holiday
🗓️Suggested: 16–20 nights

Why These Destinations Work Together

Japan and Vietnam sit at very different points on the travel spectrum, and that contrast is exactly what makes the combination so rewarding. Japan's exceptional infrastructure and polished hospitality are quite different from Vietnam's warmth, spontaneity and vibrant street life. Travelling between them keeps the entire trip energised — there's no risk of the journey feeling repetitive. Connecting flights are straightforward, typically via Hong Kong, Bangkok or Taipei, with flight times of around four to six hours.

Example Itinerary

1

Japan — 8–10 nights

Begin in Japan, where the gap between expectation and reality almost always runs in your favour. Tokyo is the essential starting point, before heading south-west by bullet train.

  • Arrive in Tokyo — explore Shibuya, Shinjuku and the quieter Yanaka district
  • Visit Teamlab Borderless or Planets for a uniquely Japanese immersive art experience
  • Take the Shinkansen to Kyoto and visit Fushimi Inari, Kinkakuji and the Arashiyama bamboo grove
  • Stay a night in a traditional ryokan inn with tatami rooms and kaiseki dinner
  • Take a day trip to Nara to meet the famous free-roaming deer
  • End with a night in Osaka — explore Dotonbori and eat generously from the street food stalls
2

Vietnam — 8–10 nights

Fly from Japan to Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City and settle into a completely different rhythm. Vietnam's pace is faster and more chaotic, but its warmth is immediate.

  • Arrive in Hanoi — explore the Old Quarter on foot and visit the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum
  • Take an overnight cruise through Ha Long Bay — one of the world's great natural wonders
  • Travel south to Hoi An — explore the lantern-lit Ancient Town and commission a tailor
  • Spend a morning at An Bang Beach, Hoi An's quieter and less developed coastline
  • Explore the imperial citadel and royal tombs of nearby Hue on a day trip
  • End in Ho Chi Minh City — visit the War Remnants Museum and eat pho at dawn on the pavement

Highlights of Each Destination

Japan

🗼

Tokyo

The world's most vibrant city — from Shibuya's crossing to the quiet neighbourhoods of Shimokitazawa.

⛩️

Kyoto's Temples

More than 1,600 Buddhist temples and shrines, including the iconic Fushimi Inari gate tunnel.

🌸

Cherry Blossom Season

Hanami — the Japanese art of blossom viewing — transforms parks and temple gardens every spring.

🍣

Japanese Food Culture

From ramen to sushi to kaiseki, Japan's food culture is one of the most refined and diverse in the world.

Vietnam

🚢

Ha Long Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage landscape of 1,600 limestone islands rising from emerald-green water.

🏮

Hoi An

A UNESCO-listed trading port glowing with lanterns — full of tailors, galleries and excellent restaurants.

🍲

Vietnamese Cuisine

Street food that rivals anywhere — from Hanoi pho to Hoi An white rose dumplings.

🌾

Sapa and the North

Spectacular rice terraces and Hmong hill tribe villages in the mountains near the Chinese border.

Best Time to Travel

Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are ideal for both destinations. Japan's cherry blossoms peak in late March to mid-April. Vietnam is at its most comfortable in spring and autumn across most of the country, avoiding the summer heat and the winter rains in the north.
