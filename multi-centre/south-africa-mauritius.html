Where nature, culture and luxury meet

The South Africa and Mauritius combination is the definitive safari-and-beach pairing — one that travel writers, honeymooners and experienced travellers return to again and again. South Africa offers one of the most complete travel experiences in the world: the Big Five in Kruger, the dramatic beauty of the Cape Peninsula, world-class wine in Stellenbosch and the extraordinary cultural richness of a country with a complex and fascinating history. Then Mauritius provides the perfect contrast — a luxurious Indian Ocean island with pristine lagoons, five-star resorts and a gentler pace of life that feels like a reward very well earned.

✈️Two-Centre Holiday
🗓️Suggested: 17–21 nights

Why These Destinations Work Together

South Africa and Mauritius work beautifully together because the combination satisfies two very different kinds of travel. The first half is active, stimulating and genuinely moving — whether you're tracking lions at dawn in Kruger or gazing from the summit of Table Mountain. The second half is about rest, beauty and pure indulgence. Flights between South Africa and Mauritius are short and frequent, typically around four hours from Johannesburg, making the transition between the two feel completely effortless.

Example Itinerary

1

South Africa — 10–12 nights

South Africa demands time — there is simply too much to experience in a short visit. This phase combines a private safari with Cape Town and the Winelands.

  • Arrive in Johannesburg and transfer to a private game lodge in the Kruger area
  • Twice-daily game drives over three to four nights — tracking the Big Five with an expert guide
  • Fly to Cape Town and ascend Table Mountain by cable car at golden hour
  • Drive the Cape Peninsula — Cape of Good Hope, Boulder's Beach penguins, Chapman's Peak
  • Spend two nights in Franschhoek or Stellenbosch, tasting wines at world-class estates
  • End with a sunset cruise in Cape Town harbour or a final evening at the V&A Waterfront
2

Mauritius — 7–9 nights

Fly from Cape Town to Mauritius in around five hours and check into your beach resort. The pace changes the moment you land.

  • Settle into your resort on the west or north coast and swim in the Indian Ocean lagoon
  • Take a glass-bottom boat trip over Blue Bay Marine Park — one of Africa's finest marine reserves
  • Book a full-day catamaran cruise with swimming stops and a fresh seafood lunch
  • Visit the Seven Coloured Earths at Chamarel and the Chamarel Rum Distillery
  • Hike Le Morne Brabant for panoramic views across the southern lagoon
  • Spend a final evening watching the sun set over the water from your resort terrace

Highlights of Each Destination

South Africa

🦁

Kruger National Park

One of Africa's great safari destinations — home to the Big Five and some extraordinary private game lodges.

🏔️

Table Mountain

Cape Town's flat-topped landmark — one of the world's great cable car rides with panoramic views.

🍷

Cape Winelands

Stellenbosch and Franschhoek produce world-class wines in scenery of extraordinary beauty.

🌊

Cape Peninsula

A dramatic drive south of Cape Town — wild beaches, penguins at Boulders and the Cape of Good Hope.

Mauritius

🐟

Blue Bay Marine Park

One of the finest marine parks in Africa, with exceptional coral gardens and snorkelling.

🌋

Le Morne Brabant

A dramatic UNESCO World Heritage basalt monolith — cultural symbol and excellent hiking destination.

⛵

Catamaran Cruising

Full-day lagoon cruises with swimming stops and fresh seafood are a Mauritius highlight.

🍹

Rum Heritage

The Chamarel distillery produces award-winning rum in a spectacular hillside setting.

Best Time to Travel

October to April works well for both destinations. South Africa's Cape Town and Western Cape are in summer — warm, dry and spectacular. Mauritius is entering its warmer season, with the clearest lagoons of the year. For Kruger specifically, May to September offers the best game viewing conditions with lower vegetation.
